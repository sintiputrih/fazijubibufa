My Little Pony Equestria Girls Movies In Order

My Little Pony Equestria Girls movies in order are Equestria Girls, Rainbow Rocks, Friendship Games, and Legend of Everfree.

1. Equestria Girls (2013)

My Little Pony Equestria Girls is Hasbro’s toy line’s first installment.

The movie follows Princess Twilight Sparkle, who is about to be crowned as a princess of Equestria, but Princess Celestia’s former student Sunset Shimmer steals it.

To retrieve her stolen crown, which contains the Element of Magic, Sparkle goes to an alternate world in a human teenage form.

On her journey, she meets human pony friends, who help her searching the crown before the portal closes for thirty moons.

The 2013 Flash animated fantasy musical film Equestria Girls received mixed critical reviews as critics pointed out the plot, character design, and writing.

  • IMDb: 6.2/10
  • Rotten Tomatoes: 74% audience score
  • Available on: Vudu and Prime Video

2. Rainbow Rocks (2014)

Rainbow Rocks follows the journey of Princess Twilight Sparkle to save Canterlot High School from the siren trio the Dazzlings from Equestria.

After defecting Sunset Shimmer, Canterlot High School’s former bully, Sparkle returns to the parallel world again.

This time it is not about her crown but to contest in the school’s upcoming musical showcase, Battle of the Bands, alongside her human counter friends band Rainbooms.

The movie received positive critical reception from both critics and viewers.

Overall, they believed this sequel was far better than the first in practically every aspect.

  • IMDb: 6.9/10
  • Rotten Tomatoes: 85% audience score
  • Available on: Vudu and Prime Video

3. Friendship Games (2015)

The third installment in the Equestria Girls franchise Friendship Games again takes on a magical journey to Canterlot High School.

The 2015 film follows the sports competition The Friendship Game between Canterlot High School and Crystal Prep Academy.

While the human counterpart of Pony starts preparing for the contest, they find the other Sparkle performing from their rival team.

Meanwhile, viewers thought the Friendship Games established its identity and didn’t rely heavily on the Friendship is Magic series.

  • IMDb: 6.5/10
  • Rotten Tomatoes: 69% audience score
  • Available on: Prime Video and AppleTV+

4. Legend of Everfree (2016)

Legend of Everfree is the last Equestria Girls movie centered on the field trip where Twilight struggles with her telekinetic powers.

When the Canterlot High School student joined a trip to a nature camp, Camo Everfree, Twilight believed her powers were the reason for the recent accident.

While she tried to avoid it and flee from the camo, Sunset supported and helped to uncover the truth behind the mysterious magical force.

Viewers mentioned the third sequel is not as good as ‘Rainbow Rocks’ but a huge improvement over the first film.

  • IMDb: 6.4/10
  • Rotten Tomatoes: 60% audience score
  • Available on: Prime Video and AppleTV+

Old My Little Pony Movies In Order

Old My Pony Movies in order are A Very Minty Christmas, The Princess Promenade, and Twinkle Wish Adventure.

My Little Pony: The Movie (1986)

My Little Pony The Movie is based on My Little Pony, Hasbro toy line, which follows the little ponies at Dream Castle.

When they were organizing a festival to celebrate spring, Hydai, an evil witch, oversees the event.

As she becomes disgusted by the ponies’ enjoyment, she orders her daughters, Draggle and Reeka, to ruin their fun.

After her daughter’s unsuccessful attempt, the witch plans to turn the idyllic Ponyville into a wasteland with a horrifying substance known as the Smooze.

The critics widely criticized the movie, and Nina Darnton from The New York Times added the film does not carry the element that would move young viewers.

  • IMDb: 5.9/10
  • Rotten Tomatoes: 72% audience score
  • Voice actors: Danny DeVito, Madeline Kahn, Rhea Perlman, Cloris Leachman, and Tony Randall
  • Director: Rhea Perlman

My Little Pony: A Very Minty Christmas (2005)

My Little Pony: A Very Minty Christmas is centered on Minty’s journey to save Christmas.

When Minty runs the ‘Here Comes Christmas Candy Cane’ accidentally, which directs Santa Claus to Ponyville.

Meanwhile, she attempts to account for her wrongdoing and offers each pony her socks. As it was not the ultimate solution, Pinkie Pie suggested she go to the North Pole to make it right.

The first titular toy franchise, A Very Minty Christmas, gained positive acclaim from critics. Viewers praised the music and artwork, mentioning it as one of the greatest Christmas specials.

  • IMDb: 5/10
  • Rotten Tomatoes: 56% audience score
  • Voice actors: Tabitha St. Germain, Janyse Jaud, Venus Terzo, Lenore Zann
  • Director: Vic Dal Chele
  • Available on: Hulu

My Little Pony: The Princess Promenade (2006)

The Princess Promenade is the second feature film and the first one to encourage the Crystal Princess line.

While the ponies prepare for the Spring Promenade with the Breezies’ help, the troublesome weed lands Wysteria and makes her princess.

Before My Little Pony: Friendship Is Magic debut, it was the first successful franchise of My Little Pony animation.

  • IMDb: 7.5/10
  • Rotten Tomatoes: 80% audience score
  • Voice actors: Tabitha St. Germain, Janyse Jaud, Venus Terzo
  • Director: Vic Dal Chele
  • Available on: Hulu and Prime Video

My Little Pony: Twinkle Wish Adventure (2009)

Twinkle Wish Adventure follows the pony’s journey to discover the Wishing Star.

In Ponyville, all the ponies are excited to watch Twinkle Wish in the upcoming Winter Wishes Festival.

Before the festival, dragon Whimsey Weatherbe kidnapped the Twinkle Wish. As a result, Pinkie Pie and her other pony friends went to find it before the night of the festival.

  • IMDb: 4.7/10
  • Rotten Tomatoes: 57% audience score
  • Voice actors: Janyse Jaud, Anna Cummer, Chantal Strand, Andrea Libman
