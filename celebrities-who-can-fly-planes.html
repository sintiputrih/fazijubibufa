Celebrities Who Can Fly Planes

Angelina Jolie

Angelina Jolie became a licensed pilot in 2004 and has earned her instrument rating and commercial pilot's license. She is qualified to fly single-engine and multi-engine planes, and has been known to fly a Cirrus SR22 and a Cessna 208 Caravan. Jolie has utilized her piloting skills for humanitarian efforts, flying herself to refugee camps and disaster areas in countries such as Cambodia, Sudan, and Haiti. She has also worked with the United Nations High Commissioner for Refugees to promote the use of aviation in humanitarian efforts.

Tom Cruise

Tom Cruise, in addition to being a Hollywood superstar, is a passionate aviator. He obtained his private pilot's license in 1994 and has acquired multiple certifications since then. Cruise is qualified to fly a variety of aircraft, including the World War II-era P-51 Mustang and the Pitts Special S-2B aerobatic plane. Known for performing his own stunts in movies, he has piloted his own plane in the film "American Made." Cruise's dedication to aviation extends beyond the big screen, as he has advocated for aviation safety and training and was appointed as the spokesperson for the International Air Transport Association's "Cabin Safety" campaign.

Kurt Russell

Kurt Russell, a celebrated actor, is also a skilled pilot with over 5,000 hours of flying experience. He received his pilot's license in the early 1980s and has earned multiple certifications, including his multi-engine rating. Russell is qualified to fly both single-engine and multi-engine aircraft and has been known to fly a Beechcraft Bonanza, a Cessna Citation, a De Havilland Canada DHC-2 Beaver, and a Piaggio Avanti 180. His passion for aviation has led him to incorporate it into both his personal and professional life.

Jimmy Buffett

Singer and restaurant owner Jimmy Buffett fulfilled his childhood dream of learning how to fly. Today, he owns six planes and flies himself to various destinations.

Harrison Ford

The iconic actor Harrison Ford made his dream of flying a jet plane a reality when he obtained his pilot's license at the age of 52. Since then, he has worked with the Aviation Owners and Pilots Association and volunteered as a rescue pilot.

Gisele Bundchen

Supermodel Gisele Bundchen became a certified pilot in 2009, even earning her pilot's license when she was seven months pregnant. Her inspiration stemmed from wanting to know how to fly in case something went wrong during flights to shoots, where there was usually only one pilot in the plane.

John Travolta

Actor John Travolta earned his pilot license at 22 and fulfilled his dream of flying. He even has his own personal airport at his home, where he keeps his five airplanes. He is certified to fly 11 different types of aircraft, including a Boeing 737 commercial jet and certain military jets.

Tim McGraw

Country singer Tim McGraw is not only a certified pilot but a Cirrus pilot. He enjoys flying his customized SR22 to unwind and relax, even flying to events and engagements with friends.

Morgan Freeman

Actor and narrator Morgan Freeman learned how to fly when he was 65, proving that it's never too late to enjoy airplane flying experiences. He had a love of flying since his days in the Air Force when he was a mechanic.

Others

Other celebrities such as Tom Cruise, Amelia Earhart, Brad Pitt, and more have also pursued their dreams of flying and have become certified pilots, each with their unique stories and motivations.

Numerous celebrities have pursued their passion for flying by becoming certified pilots, showcasing their dedication to aviation beyond their successful careers in the entertainment industry.
