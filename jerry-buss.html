Jerry Buss Bio

Jerry Buss Early Life and Education

Jerry Buss, whose full name is Gerald Hatten Buss, was born on January 27, 1933, in Salt Lake City, Utah, USA. He grew up to become a businessman, investor, chemist, and philanthropist. But before he became the influential figure we know him as, he started his journey in a rather different field. Buss initially worked as a chemist for the Bureau of Mines. However, his ambition and drive would soon lead him down a different path.

Jerry Buss Family and Relationships

Together, they had four children: Johnny, Jim, Jeanie, and Janie. However, Jerry’s family extended beyond his immediate circle. He had six children in total, including Joey and Jesse, from his relationship with girlfriend Karen Demel. These two children also became involved with the Lakers organization, ensuring that the Buss family remained a vital part of the team’s legacy.

Jerry Buss Cause of Death

Jerry Buss passed away due to kidney failure, which was a result of ongoing health issues. He died on February 18, 2013, in Los Angeles, California, at the age of 80.

The Path to Success

Jerry Buss was not content with being just a chemist. He had bigger dreams and an entrepreneurial spirit that couldn’t be contained. He ventured into real estate investment, and it turned out to be a decision that would change his life. Alongside his business partner, Frank Mariani, Buss formed Mariani-Buss Associates, a real estate investment company. This move catapulted him to success.

In 1979, Buss made a significant purchase when he acquired Pickford, the Beverly Hills estate. His real estate ventures proved to be immensely profitable. He also dipped his toes into the world of entertainment by producing the movie “Black Eye” in 1974. Jerry Buss wasn’t just a one-dimensional businessman; he had a diverse portfolio.

Jerry Buss Challenges and Health Battles

Jerry Buss faced his fair share of challenges, both personally and health-wise. In 2012, he had to be hospitalized for several months due to an undisclosed intestinal issue. Tragically, it was revealed that he had been battling cancer since that same year. Despite his struggles, he continued to fight and persevere.

However, the battle against illness took its toll, and Jerry Buss passed away on February 18, 2013, in Los Angeles, California, at the age of 80. He succumbed to kidney failure as a result of his ongoing health issues. His death marked the end of an era in the basketball world.
