Bobby Knight Wikipedia, Who is Bobby Knight?

Bobby Knight has been listed on Wikipedia, and the below paragraphs will give you exclusive details of his bio, just as it is on Wikipedia.

Bobby Knight began playing organized basketball at Orrville High School and continued at Ohio State in 1958, when he played for Basketball Hall of Fame coach Fred Taylor. Despite being a star player in high school, he played a reserve role as a forward on the 1960 Ohio State Buckeyes team that won the NCAA Championship and featured future Hall of Fame players John Havlicek and Jerry Lucas.

The Buckeyes lost to the Cincinnati Bearcats in each of the next two NCAA Championship games, of which Knight was also a part. Due to the star power of those Ohio State teams, Knight usually received scant playing time, but that did not prevent him from making an impact. In the 1961 NCAA Championship game, Knight came off the bench with 1:41 on the clock, and Cincinnati led Ohio State, 61-59.

After graduating from Ohio State University in 1962, he coached junior varsity basketball at Cuyahoga Falls High School in Ohio for one year. Bobby Knight then enlisted in the U.S. Army and served on active duty from June 1963 to June 1965 and in the Army Reserves from June 1965 to May 1969. In 1971, Indiana University hired Bobby Knight as head coach. During his 29 years at the school, the Hoosiers won 662 games, including 22 seasons of 20 or more wins, while losing 239, a .735 winning percentage. In 24 NCAA tournament appearances at Indiana, Hoosier teams under Knight won 42 of 63 games (.667), winning titles in 1976, 1981, and 1987, while losing in the semi-finals in 1973 and 1992.

In 1972, Bobby Knight’s second year as coach, Indiana won the Big Ten championship and reached the Final Four but lost to UCLA, which was on its way to its seventh consecutive national title. The following season, 1973–74, Indiana once again captured a Big Ten title. In the two following seasons, 1974–75 and 1975–76, the Hoosiers were undefeated in the regular season and won 37 consecutive Big Ten games, including two more Big Ten championships.

The 1974–75 Hoosiers swept the entire Big Ten by an average of 22.8 points per game. However, in an 83-82 win against Purdue, they lost consensus All-American forward Scott May to a broken left arm.
