Age, Biography and Wiki

Manuel Velasco Coello is a Mexican politician who served as Governor of Chiapas from 2012 to 2018. He is a member of the Institutional Revolutionary Party (PRI). He was born on April 7, 1980 in Tuxtla Gutiérrez, Chiapas, Mexico. Velasco Coello studied law at the Autonomous University of Chiapas and obtained a master's degree in public administration from the Autonomous Technological Institute of Mexico. He began his political career in 2006, when he was elected to the Chamber of Deputies of Mexico. In 2012, he was elected Governor of Chiapas, becoming the youngest governor in Mexico. He was re-elected in 2015. In 2018, he resigned as Governor of Chiapas to run for the Senate of Mexico. He was elected to the Senate in July 2018. In 2012, Velasco ran on a PRI-PVEM coalition ticket in the 2012 gubernatorial election and won, becoming Chiapas' governor later that year.

In 2003, he was elected Deputy of the LIX Legislature of the Mexican Congress representing Chiapas. At the age of 23, he became the youngest person to serve as a federal deputy. He left his Chamber seat to run for Senate, winning Chiapas' seat in the July elections. At the age of 26, Velasco set another history by becoming the youngest Senator in Mexican history.

Manuel Velasco Coello (born 7 April 1980) is a Mexican politician and a member of PVEM. He served as the Governor of Chiapas from 2012 to 2018. He was the first PVEM politician to become a governor. Prior to his governorship, he served as a state deputy, Federal deputy and Senator, each time becoming the youngest person to ever hold such office.
