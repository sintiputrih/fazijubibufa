Kiumars Pourahmad Wife: Who Is Mehraneh Rabi?

Kiumars Pourahmad, an Iranian director, screenwriter, editor, and producer, passed away on April 5, 2023. He started his career as a film reviewer and assistant director for TV shows in the early 1970s. Following the production of a few short films, he made his first feature picture, Tatureh, in 1983.

Children and teenagers who are facing difficulties are the main characters in the bulk of Kiumars Pourahmad’s films. “Unfinished Childhood,” a book on Pourahmad’s life, was also published. His writing and directing are best known for the films Where Are My Shoes? (1996), The Double Lottie (1996), and Night Bus (2007).

On March 5, 2010, during the annual Donyaye Tasvir movie ceremony, Kiumars Pourahmad called for the freedom of Jafar Panahi, Mohammad Rasoulof, and Mehdi Pourmoussa while also praising his coworkers. Rassoulof and Pourmoussa were released from the Evin prison on March 17, 2010, however Panahi remains confined in ward 209.
