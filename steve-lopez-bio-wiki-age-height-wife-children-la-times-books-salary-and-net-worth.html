Steve Lopez Biography

Steve Lopez is an American journalist born on October 15, 1953, in Pittsburg, California as Steven M. Lopez. Lopez has been a columnist for The Angeles Times since 2001. His book The Soloist inspired the 2009 film of the same name, he joined the staff of the Los Angeles Times in May 2001 after four years at Time Inc. He wrote for Time, Sports Illustrated, Life as well as Entertainment Weekly.

    Steve Lopez Career

    Lopez is a native of Pittsburg, California, as well as attended San Jose State University. Lopez has been on staff at Time Inc. as well as written for Time, Life, Entertainment Weekly, and Sports Illustrated. In addition, Lopez was on staff at The Philadelphia Inquirer, the San Jose Mercury News, and the Oakland Tribune.

    Lopez wrote the novels Third and Indiana, The Sunday Macaroni Club, and In the Clear. Lopez has also compiled a collection of his works from The Philadelphia Inquirer titled Land of Giants, as well as a collection of Los Angeles Times columns called Dreams & Schemes.

    His series of columns about his unlikely relationship with a cellist with schizophrenia Nathaniel Anthony Ayers became the subject of a national best-selling book by Steve that inspired the film The Soloist, which stars Robert Downey Jr. as Lopez. He was interviewed in the 2007 documentary Skid Row.

    Ayers and Lopez’s relationship was also nationally highlighted in the March 22, the year 2009, episode of 60 Minutes.

    In October 2010, Los Angeles city attorney Carmen Trutanich invited him to participate in a driving test while under the influence of marijuana, the purpose of this study was to gauge the potential ramifications of California Proposition 19.
